‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

Is running a .onion site legal?

Yes, absolutely. The Tor network and .onion services are legal tools for privacy protection. Like any technology, they can be used for both legitimate and illegal purposes. We're demonstrating and promoting legitimate educational uses.

Why should educators care about .onion sites?

.onion sites offer: 1) Complete privacy for readers in censored regions, 2) Zero cost publishing without domains or hosting, 3) Censorship resistance for sensitive educational content, and 4) Real-world technology education about privacy tools.

What about performance? Aren't .onion sites slow?

Tor does add latency, but for static educational content (like this site), performance is excellent. Our Docker+Nginx setup is optimized for speed with compression, caching, and modern web practices. The calculator demo proves complex interactions work smoothly.

Can I really use this for free forever?

Yes. The only requirement is a computer (even a Raspberry Pi) to run Docker. No domain registration fees, no hosting bills. Your .onion address is free and automatically generated. For 24/7 availability, you might want a $5/month VPS, but even that's optional.
